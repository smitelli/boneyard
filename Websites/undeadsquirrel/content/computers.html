Computers
 "I run Win98 SE on a 66 MHz!"

I'm sure that you've all figured out that I collect computers. I am proud of the horribly obsolete "flock" that I have collected over the years. Not because they're especially fast, or because they have been blessed by the hand of the good Lord... No, it is just the simple fact that I have collected 30 (quite possibly more) computers since 1993. I have opened and upgraded them all. I hope to share some of this with you now, if you even care anymore.

Here they are, in nearly the order I got them.

IBM Personal System/2 Model 55SX
This was the first electronic device my family owned that could be classified as a "computer." I got it in mid-1993. It was used for some mundane task at Atlantic City Medical Center (ACMC) mainland division. They threw it out, my dad got it from the trash, and it became our computer. Such humble beginnings. I'm not sure of the upgrade status of the machine before we got it, what came stock, what was added on, etc. What I am sure of, however, is what I'll tell you about it.

  • CPU: Intel 386SX @ 16 MHz
  • RAM: 4 MB (72-pin)
  • Hard Drive: 60 MB ESDI internal
  • Floppy Drives: 3.5" (1.44 MB) internal, 5.25" (1.2 MB) external
  • Video: IBM VGA adapter
  • Modem: 2400 baud Ventel externel
  • Mouse: Microsoft PS/2 Bus Mouse
  • Additional Hardware: Logitech ScanMan 4" black and white handheld scanner
  • BIOS Features: Can boot IBM ROM BASIC
  • Operating System: IBM DOS 5.00

"Homemade 800" - no official model name
This is my primary computer. It was built from parts from the JDR catalog in June of 1999. Here is the configuration when it was first powered on:

  • CPU: Intel Pentium II @ 333 MHz (66 MHz FSB x 5.0)
  • RAM: 64 MB PC66 (168 pin, one DIMM)
  • Power Supply: 235 watt ATX
  • Hard Drive: Western Digital Caviar 310200 (10 GB) Ultra DMA66
  • CD-ROM: Memorex Generic 36x IDE
  • Video Card: STB GLyderMAX-2 AGP 8 MB
  • Sound: Avance Logic 16-bit ISA
  • Modem: Creative Modem Blaster Flash56 DI5601 ISA
  • BIOS Manufacturer: American Megatrends
  • Operating System: IBM DOS 5.00
